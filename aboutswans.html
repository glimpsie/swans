Overlooking the Bay

The Mute Swans

In Sheepshead Bay, there is usually an assortment of birds that flock to the bay’s water either to hunt fish or eat breadcrumbs thrown by well-meaning people visiting the birds. Aside from the clouds of seagulls, migrating geese and the occasional duck, no bird is more linked to that area than the graceful swans. Introduced over a century ago to NY area, the swans have been residents of Sheepshead Bay for a few decades. They reside in the water and swim around boats and buoys, many feet below the street level only to be admired, but too far out of human reach. The attraction seems to be mutual as the swans swim towards people that throw bread to them, they often display their skill of spreading one wing at a time while stretching, or forming a circle with wings half raised, or flying so low that it appears as if they are running on water to delight of children and adults alike. The swans feed on the fish and other vegetation in the bay and are mute, but not completely flightless. They live in the bay year round, and can easily be seen unless there are cases of bad weather. They have anywhere from three to eight cygnets and while the adults are white, the babies and adolescents are dark gray. While swans and other large water fowl are considered aggressive and territorial, they will only attack when provoked. In recent years, due to their status as introduced species, there has been movements by the local government to either control their population or completely exterminate them. However, due to outrage by the Sheepshead Bay population, these forms of population control are shifting to somewhat more humane ones like separating males from females and destroying their nests and coating the eggs in oil. It is already apparent the number of swans has decreased drastically and there are no dark grey teenage swans who used to be seen timidly catching bread pieces. The government’s goal is to eliminate the swans by 2015 and it looks like this is a goal they will achieve.

The Holocaust Memorial Park

Overlooking the Sheepshead Bay is a Holocaust Memorial to millions of murdered Jews in Europe during World War II. The memorial underscores the preservation of life. How ironic, it is now also a place where the swans are being completely exterminated, although, this extermination will be achieved in a humane way.
